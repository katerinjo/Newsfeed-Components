Rabbit Science

May 30th, 2019

The particular example that popped into my head that year, was researchers injecting tiny needles into rabbit hamster brains (we thought it was lions for some bizarre philosophical reason). The hamsters lived 20 weeks and then went away, and the researchers were done, leaving the rabbit’s ancestors in complete shock and the rabbit brain (which had specialized neural wiring for information representation) dying and being replaced by a nonfunctional but (they hoped?) more inclusive-seeming rabbit (this being a goalist’s dream, really). Experimental results in this case were noisy, but so far as I could tell - assuming the researcher actually lived and observed the hamster survival - there was no sign of the microorganisms killing the rabbit.

Why are we giving prosthetic limbs to people with Phantom Limb Syndrome? Isn't this just enabling mental illness? Sorry but biologically you don't have an arm.

April 23rd, 2019

Whatever though if you want to pay for it yourself that's up to you but don't go expecting me to call your mechanically attached pointing device an "arm".

Some guy at my work has a pointing stick and he keeps saying things like "I'm glad I got my new arm". I always correct him when he says this. No, Ben, that is NOT an arm. One time I asked for someone to lend me a hand with some boxes and he reached over and it touched me. I kinda freaked out and ranted at him for a bit, like, I asked for a hand, that is not a hand, scientifically it simply is not a hand.

My friends keep telling me that I'm being "ableist" or something when I talk about this but I'm not, I don't hate these people, in fact I only want what's best for them (removing their fake limbs and committing them to a mental health hospital), and what's best just happens to be what is easiest for me to process.

A High Evolutionary Lad

May 30th, 2019

I once read an SF story where a “High Evolutionary Lad” averts a nuclear war by 80% evolution, against the further predicted predictions of science. (The author being careful to make this possible while still affirming its SFAM merit.) The High Evolutionary Lad is, I expected, going to some considerable length to explain why this was reasonable and necessary. He makes no secret of the fact that he does not believe in evolution, and gives explicit arguments why it is unreasonable to expect anything to become real. He even wore an Evolutionary Hat (an apparent expression of his own personal style, as a fun Fact to tease out of whatever quarks he was touching). The author, in his admirable caution, tried to make the High Evolutionary Lad sound like he believed in something - a message, if ever there was one.
